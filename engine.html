The Steam Engine

Steam engines are what power steamboats, factories, and locomotives. They utilize boiling water and hot steam to push a piston back and forth and power a machine. Steam-powered machines proved to be reliable, easy to use anywhere, and powerful.

The steam engine was created by Thomas Savery in 1698. 80 years later, a man named James Watt made heavy improvements to the power and speed of the steam engine, making it much more useful; a unit of power called the Watt was named after him. Steam engines are still in use today.
